Activity A · Module 2

The limitation gallery

6 real AI outputs, each with a different type of failure. Your task: identify what went wrong — and what student knowledge would be needed to catch it.

Read each AI output carefully. Select the failure type you think best describes the problem, then read the explanation. Some failures are subtle — that is the point. If AI always failed obviously, students would catch it themselves.
Activity B · Module 2

The AI capability map — combined activity

Two parts: first sort tasks into what AI does well vs poorly, then rate each task by how central it is to disciplinary learning. The intersection reveals your redesign priorities.

Part 1: Sort the tasks below into two columns — AI does this well, or AI struggles with this. Trust your instincts first, then check. Part 2: Once sorted, you will rate how central each task is to disciplinary learning. The combination produces your personal risk map.
